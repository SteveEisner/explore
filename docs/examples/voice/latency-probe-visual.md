## Chapter summary

This explainer shows a voice-driven pipeline that can both understand and act. You look at it to learn what happens from microphone to spoken reply, and to see that the system can change the workspace while you’re still talking.

## User flow

- Mic: your voice is captured and sent out immediately from the browser.
- Listening: the system waits for a complete thought, not just silence.
- Words: speech becomes text, and text becomes intent.
- Hands: the intent can trigger actions like opening pages, editing the wiki, or rebuilding artifacts.
- Speech: the system answers out loud, and you can interrupt at any time.

The page may update before the spoken answer finishes, because the workspace can change while the conversation continues.

## Maintainer view

- Capture: audio goes directly from the browser to the realtime model; the server only mints a short-lived token and does not relay audio.
- Semantic VAD: turn detection fires when an utterance reads as complete, which makes interruption feel natural.
- Transcript: voice utterances are recorded with app state in the same feedback flow as other channels.
- Tool bridge: tool actions travel over the existing connection to perform wiki edits, artifact edits, state changes, or heavier rebuilds.
- Synthesis: speech streams back over the same connection, with echo cancellation handling the user’s voice and playback being the tricky edge case.
